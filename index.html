🎯 The immersion philosophy is clear: "How you create your solution is just as important as what you create."
Our feedback shows operational gaps — pre-work timing, tech access, role clarity — that are fixable. We leave this session with named owners and firm decisions, not open questions. This is a decision meeting.
⚡ Agentic Development is Mandatory
All FDE solutions must take an agentic approach. This is not optional. Teams will build solutions using agentic patterns: tool use, RAG, multi-agent orchestration, agentic memory, and human-in-the-loop designs. The "why agentic" conversation should happen in pre-work, not Day 1.
🛠 Environment Setup: Use Kick-off Time or Pre-Session Meeting
The immersion remains 2 days. For environment setup, teams have two options: (1) Use the last 30 minutes of kick-off for tool provisioning and access verification, or (2) Meet separately as a team after kick-off but before the immersion begins. All tools (Kiro, GitHub, AVP, hyperscaler access) must be verified working before Day 1 starts.

Role Expectations
One-pager per role — What success looks like
Each role has specific expectations during the immersion. These one-pagers should be shared with participants during pre-work so they arrive knowing their contribution.
💼
Solution & Business Leadership
SO / Customer Strategy

Your Ownership

  • Viability: Is this the right problem to solve? You own the business case.
  • ROI framing: Lead the value conversation with client. Numbers don't have to be perfect — they have to be reasoned.
  • Scope ruthlessness: Cut scope to deliver value in 2 days. Protect the team from scope creep.
  • Client narrative: Own the story we tell in the final presentation.
  • Code contribution: Yes, you contribute code. This is expected, not optional.

Day 1 Focus

  • Lead problem-framing session (11:30 block)
  • Challenge assumptions — don't accept the stated problem at face value
  • Define success criteria with XD before engineering begins

Day 2 Focus

  • Final presentation narrative and ROI defense
  • Client objection handling
  • Handoff documentation for next steps
💻
Technical & Data Leadership
SE / IE / Data Engineer

Your Ownership

  • Feasibility: Can we build this? You own the technical case.
  • Agentic architecture: Design solutions using agentic patterns — this is required.
  • Rapid prototyping: Build working prototypes in hours using IDE agents (Kiro, Cursor, Windsurf).
  • Client influence: Steer clients toward higher-value solutions when their stated use case isn't the right one.
  • Integration reality: Surface data quality and integration blockers early — this is a success, not a failure.

Day 1 Focus

  • Listen during problem-framing — don't start coding yet
  • Architecture decisions based on feasibility and timeline
  • Environment verification (should be done in pre-session, but confirm)

Day 2 Focus

  • Build the working prototype
  • Technical demo in final presentation
  • Handoff documentation: architecture, dependencies, next steps
🎨
Experience Leadership
XD / Service Designer

Your Ownership

  • Desirability: Will users want and adopt this? You own the user case.
  • Problem framing: Co-lead the 11:30 block with SO. Keep user needs centered.
  • Rapid prototyping: Use AI tools to accelerate high-fidelity prototypes. Contribute code.
  • Strategic context: Capture context, decisions, and principles in documentation the team can act on.
  • Workflow design: Understand end-to-end before designing. Bring service design thinking.

Day 1 Focus

  • Co-lead problem-framing with SO (11:30 block)
  • User advocacy — keep user needs visible under time pressure
  • Early wireframes and flow definition

Day 2 Focus

  • UI/UX polish on the prototype
  • Visual storytelling in final presentation
  • User journey documentation for handoff

Immersion Expectations
What participants should know before Day 1
Categorized expectations to share during pre-work. These set the right mindset and reduce Day 1 confusion.

📚 Pre-work Expectations

  • AVP training: Complete all modules before arrival — this is mandatory
  • Hyperscaler cert: AWS Agentic AI Learning Plan or GCP Gemini Enterprise track
  • Tool access: Verify Kiro, GitHub, and cloud access work before Day 1
  • Role brief: Read your role one-pager and understand your ownership areas
  • Time commitment: Block full 2 days — no competing meetings

🎯 Mindset Expectations

  • Not a hackathon: Optimize for learning and decision quality, not polish
  • Discomfort is designed: "If you are uncomfortable, you are doing it right"
  • Client pushback is the exercise: Personas will challenge you — that's the point
  • Prototype, not production: Demonstrate value and feasibility, not completeness
  • Everyone codes: Yes, including SO and XD. AI tools make this accessible.

🤝 Collaboration Expectations

  • Role clarity: Know your ownership and stay in your lane until invited out
  • Cross-functional respect: Engineers don't code until problem-framing is complete
  • Healthy disagreement: Articulate your view and resolve through reasoning
  • Time boundaries: Respect the timeline — every block has a decision output
  • Help seeking: Ask for help early. Struggle is expected; suffering in silence is not.

📊 Outcome Expectations

  • Working prototype: Something that demonstrates value, even if incomplete
  • ROI narrative: Defensible reasoning, not perfect numbers
  • Client presentation: 10-15 minute pitch with demo
  • Handoff documentation: Architecture, decisions, and next steps captured
  • #FDETrained: Your tag gets updated within 24 hours of completion
